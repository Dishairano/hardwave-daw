# Code of Conduct

Hardwave DAW is built by people who want music software to be better. We expect everyone in this community — contributors, maintainers, users, plugin developers — to act in a way that keeps it that way.

## Standards

**Do:**

- Be direct, but be kind. You can disagree without being rude.
- Critique code, not people.
- Assume good faith on first reading. Ask before assuming malice.
- Help newer contributors. Everyone was new once.
- Credit other people's work. Don't take credit for what you didn't do.

**Don't:**

- Harass, threaten, dox, or attempt to intimidate anyone for any reason.
- Use slurs or sexualized language. This is a tool for making music; act like a professional.
- Discriminate against anyone for race, gender, sexuality, nationality, religion, disability, age, body, or appearance.
- Treat the project as a personal platform for unrelated political or commercial promotion.
- Spam, brigade, or attempt to manipulate community channels.

These rules apply in this repository, in our Discord, and in any space where you are representing Hardwave DAW.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for enforcing these standards. They will, in order of escalation:

1. **Private warning** — a quiet note explaining what crossed the line and what to fix.
2. **Public note** — a comment on the offending message marking it as out of bounds.
3. **Temporary suspension** — removal from the project space (repo, Discord) for a defined period.
4. **Permanent ban** — for repeated violations or for any single severe violation (harassment, threats, doxing, deliberate malice).

Maintainer decisions are not court rulings. They are not appealable to a third party. If you think a maintainer made the wrong call, raise it with another maintainer or in private to the project lead. Repeated bad-faith appeals are themselves a violation.

## Reporting

Email `conduct@hardwavestudios.com` with:

- What happened.
- Where it happened (link if possible).
- Anyone else involved.

Reports are read by the project lead and treated as confidential. We will not share your identity with the person you reported without your permission.

If a maintainer is the subject of the report, email the project lead directly: `daniel@hardwavestudios.com`.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub, Discord, mailing lists, in-person events, and anywhere the project is officially represented. It also applies to private interactions when those interactions affect the project or its members.
